# Contributing to CAOS

## Welcome and orientation

The CAOS community includes people with disabilities, accessibility professionals, and anyone who participates in or benefits from open source. That's pretty much everyone. We believe that good ideas, useful feedback, and meaningful participation can come from anyone who thinks and cares about these important issues. Whether you're a person with a disability, developer, advocate, researcher, open-source contributor/owner/user, or any combination thereof, we invite you to participate in CAOS. This document explains how to get involved, and explains the many different forms that involvement may take.

---

## Ways to participate

Participation is not limited to any one activity. There are many ways to become an Agent of CAOS. Options include:

- **Follow along** — all governance documents, policy discussions, and meeting records are public. Staying informed is a legitimate and valued form of participation.
- **Give feedback on the CAOS website or public materials** — tell us what works, what doesn't, and what's missing.
- **Participate in governance** — comment on open policy discussions, propose changes to existing policies, or propose new ones.
- **Volunteer** — there's lots to do. We'd love your help with a specific project, activity, or event that draws your interest. We also have dedicated working groups (also known as committees) and an Advisory Board with openings for contributors with a wide range of expertise and experience.
- **Propose a program, activity, or event** — if you have an idea for something CAOS should do, we want to hear it.
- **Engage on a partner project** — if you're involved in an existing open-source project, you can help build a bridge of accessibility knowledge and disability awareness between their community and ours.
- **Report an accessibility problem** — anywhere in CAOS's work: our website, documents, events, or governance processes.

Not sure where your idea fits? Just reach out — see [Questions and contact](#questions-and-contact) below.

## Before you contribute

We want to make sure everyone feels welcome and understands how our community operates. Before engaging as a contributor, please read the [CAOS Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and the [CAOS Accessibility Policy](policies/accessibility-policy.md). CAOS contributors are expected to understand and comply with both documents.

## GitHub for governance contributions

The [center4aos governance repository](https://github.com/center4aos) is where CAOS policy discussions happen in the open. Use GitHub when you want to:

- Comment on or ask questions about an existing policy
- Participate in an active policy discussion
- Propose a new policy or a change to an existing one
- Flag a concern about how a policy is working in practice

### Opening an issue

Opening an issue is the right first step for all governance contributions — questions, concerns, and early-stage ideas all belong here before becoming formal proposals.

The easiest way to submit an issue on an existing policy is to find it on the CAOS website and click the "Submit an Issue" link in the page footer. This automatically opens a new issue in the governance repository with the right template, pre-filled with a reference to the specific policy document. If you're comfortable using GitHub directly, you can also open an issue from the governance repository using the Policy Issue template.

To bring an issue to the attention of the full Board, mention `@center4aos/board` in your issue comment.

### Submitting a proposal

All proposals begin as issues. Once an issue discussion has reached sufficient clarity and there is a clear direction to move forward, the Policy Maintainer will give the go-ahead to open a pull request. That pull request is the formal proposal.

**For changes to an existing policy**, the pull request edits the policy file directly.

**For new policies**, the pull request adds a new document to the `/proposals/` directory using the policy template. Once approved, the Policy Maintainer moves it into the appropriate location in the repository.

The Policy Maintainer can open a pull request on behalf of contributors who are not comfortable with GitHub.

### What happens after a pull request is opened

Opening a pull request triggers the **14-day formal review period**:

1. **Formal review (14 days)** — The community reviews the draft. Subject matter reviewers (legal, accessibility, financial, or technical as relevant) are invited to comment. The Policy Maintainer monitors and facilitates. If no clear progress is made within 14 days, the pull request is closed; the originating issue may be re-opened to continue discussion before a new PR is attempted.
2. **Revision** — The proposer incorporates feedback and updates the draft.
3. **Decision** — The Board votes on major policy changes at the next regular Board meeting. Minor editorial changes may be approved by the Policy Maintainer without a Board vote.
4. **Publication** — Approved changes are merged and a new version is tagged.

Realistic timelines from pull request to published decision vary by the type of change:

- **Minor editorial changes** (clarifications, examples, formatting) — approved by the Policy Maintainer without a Board vote. Typically resolved within **2 to 3 weeks** of the PR opening.
- **Major policy changes** (new obligations, new policies, structural revisions) — require a Board vote at a regular Board meeting. The Board meets quarterly at minimum, so timing depends on where the PR lands in the meeting cycle. Expect **4 to 16 weeks** from PR opening to published decision.

Issue discussions prior to the PR have no fixed timeline.

## Accessibility of the contribution process

CAOS is committed to both accessible outputs and accessible processes.

**If GitHub is not accessible for you**, or if you prefer to participate another way, you can submit comments, questions, or proposals through our [Contact page](PLACEHOLDER) or by email to info@caos.org. We will ensure your contribution enters the public record and receives the same consideration as anything submitted through GitHub.

**If you need documents in an alternative format**, contact accessibility@caos.org and we will do our best to accommodate your request.
